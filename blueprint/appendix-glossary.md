# Appendix A – Glossary
This appendix defines the key terms, regulatory frameworks, and technical specifications utilized throughout the WE BUILD ecosystem.

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| ADR (Architectural Decision Record) | A document used to capture and justify significant technical choices. ADRs serve as the project’s "logbook" to ensure transparency regarding the rationale behind protocol and standard adoption. |
| Blueprint (D4.1 Deliverable) | The primary strategic document describing the high-level architectural patterns and the "big picture" of how the WE BUILD system components integrate. |
| ITB (Interoperability Testbed) | The primary gatekeeper and automated testing infrastructure used to prove that a technical solution is ready for real-world piloting. |
| LSP (Large Scale Pilot) | A project funded by the European Commission to test the practical implementation of the EUDI Wallet framework across various cross-border use cases. |
| WBCS (WE BUILD Conformance Specifications) | Detailed technical rules that operationalize architectural intent. Approval of a WBCS signifies a commitment from consortium partners to implement those specific interfaces. |
| WE BUILD | The consortium and project focused on pioneering the European Business Wallet and EUDI Wallet use cases to reduce "red tape" in cross-border transactions. |
| Holder | The natural or legal person that controls the digital wallet. |
| Issuer | An entity that relies on authentic sources of information to issue attestations (credentials) to a Holder. |
| Relying Party / Verifier | An entity that requests and receives attestations from a Holder to verify specific attributes or identities. |
| QTSP (Qualified Trust Service Provider) | A regulated entity providing electronic trust services (e.g., signatures, seals, or delivery services) with full legal effect under eIDAS. |
| Trust Framework | The system of rules, standards, and trust lists (based on ETSI TS 119604/119612) that ensure all actors in the ecosystem are valid and compliant. |
| EBW (European Business Wallet) | A wallet designed for economic operators to manage mandates, electronic invoices, and professional documents. |
| EBWOID (European Business Wallet Owner Identification Data) | A digital set of attributes that uniquely identifies a legal entity (the business equivalent of a PID). |
| EUDI Wallet (European Digital Identity Wallet) | The European Digital Identity Wallet, a mobile or cloud-based solution for natural persons to store and share identity data. |
| PID (Personal Identification Data) | A mandatory set of attributes issued to a natural person to uniquely identify them at Level of Assurance (LoA) High. |
| QEAA / EAA (Qualified Electronic Attribute Attestation / Electronic Attribute Attestation) | Digital credentials that prove specific attributes (e.g., professional qualifications, representation rights) with either qualified (QEAA) or non-qualified (EAA) legal status. |
| ARF (Architecture and Reference Framework) | The official document provided by the European Commission (European Digital Identity Cooperation Group (EDICG)) that specifies the functionalities and design principles of the EUDI ecosystem. |
| eIDAS (Electronic Identification, Authentication and Trust Services) | The legal framework for electronic identification and trust services for electronic transactions in the European Single Market. |
| LoA (Level of Assurance) | A classification of the degree of confidence in the claimed identity of a person (e.g., Low, Substantial, High). |
| QERDS (Qualified Electronic Registered Delivery Service) | A secure communication channel that provides legal evidence of the handling of transmitted data. |
| SD-JWT (Selective Disclosure JSON Web Token) | A format allowing holders to share only specific parts of a credential while keeping other data private. |
| WSCA / WSCD (Wallet Secure Cryptographic Application / Wallet Secure Cryptographic Device) | The hardware or software environment used to manage cryptographic keys securely within the wallet. |
